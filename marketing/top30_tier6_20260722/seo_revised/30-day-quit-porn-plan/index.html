KEEP RESTARTING? BUILD A 30-DAY PLAN THAT HAS A JOB AT 11:40 PM.

A restart is not a plan. Start with the time, route, and reason that normally beats you, then decide what you will do before that moment arrives.

The direct answer: Use 30 days to build a repeatable response, not a perfect streak story. Pick a first milestone, name your Weak Window, decide the first move, and make the same response easy to reach every day.

1. Name the weak window

Do not write a vague promise to quit. Write the stretch that keeps taking the night: the room, time, device, route, and reason. Specific detail gives you something you can change.

2. Choose tonight's first move

Decide one physical action before the weak window arrives: move the phone, leave the room, turn on the lights, or contact a real person. The move should be specific enough to perform without negotiating.

3. Rehearse Fortify once

Practice the first move while the pressure is low. When the pattern starts later, Fortify puts one clear instruction first so you can act before the loop gathers momentum.

4. Review the Campaign Report

Record completed orders, resets, and what worked so the next plan improves. A bad day becomes information for the next action, not a reason to disappear.
