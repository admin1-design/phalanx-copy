PHALANX/Guides/post-slip reset

ONE SLIP IS A MOMENT. A BINGE IS A ROUTE YOU CAN INTERRUPT.

The fastest way to lose the next day is to turn one slip into a verdict. Do not. Break the sequence, make the next move physical, and return to the plan before shame gets another turn.

The direct answer: Close everything. Put the phone away. Then log the moment as a reset, not a verdict, and choose one adjustment for the next weak window.

Get PHALANX on iPhone

1. End the route

Close the site or app, put distance between you and the device, and leave the position that kept the sequence going. The goal is to stop the next click, not to win an argument about the past.

2. Put the phone away

Create physical distance before you analyze anything. Move the device to another room or a visible shared place and let the immediate pressure fall.

3. Log the reset, not a verdict

Open Return to Command after you are stable. Record the moment without shame, then choose one adjustment to the time, route, device, or first move.

4. Return to the 30-day plan

The Campaign Report preserves the useful record. You are not erasing the plan; you are using the reset to make the next weak-window response more useful.

FAQ

How do I stop one slip becoming a binge?

Close everything. Put the phone away. Then log it as a reset, not a verdict, and choose one adjustment for the next weak window.

Should I throw away my plan after a slip?

No. A slip resets the current streak; earned rank remains. Use the moment to adjust the next weak-window plan.

What does the Campaign Report do after a slip?

The Campaign Report records what worked and what to change next. It is not a public recovery feed.
